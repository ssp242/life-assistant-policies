Privacy Policy

Life Assistant · Last updated: 2026-06-17

Life Assistant is a personal-use tool built and operated by a single individual (the "developer") for the developer's own private use. It is not offered to the public and has exactly one user: the developer.

What data is accessed

With the developer's own Google account consent, the app accesses:

  • Gmail — reads unread inbox messages (sender, subject, snippet) to summarise them, and can send email on the developer's behalf when explicitly requested and confirmed by the developer.
  • Google Calendar — reads upcoming events and can create new events when explicitly requested and confirmed by the developer.

Where data is stored

All data (OAuth tokens, message history, derived summaries) is stored locally on the developer's own personal server. It is never sold, shared with advertisers, or made available to any third party for marketing purposes.

Third-party processing

To generate natural-language summaries and replies, relevant text (e.g. email subject lines/snippets, calendar event details, and the developer's own chat messages) is sent to the Anthropic Claude API (anthropic.com) for processing. This is the only third-party service involved. See Anthropic's privacy policy for how they handle data sent to their API.

Data retention & deletion

Data is retained locally for as long as the developer continues to use the tool. The developer can revoke the app's access to their Google account at any time via Google Account › Security › Third-party access, which immediately invalidates all stored tokens.
